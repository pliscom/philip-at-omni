Case Study
How Philip Liscom cut analytics turnaround from hours to 30 minutes — and saved his team a full day each week

Intuit APAC · Sydney, Australia · 350+ employees in region

The challenge

Intuit's APAC sales and finance leaders were drowning in ad hoc data requests. Every question — rep performance, pipeline health, churn by segment — required a data analyst to manually pull, clean, and format data. Weekly leadership reporting alone consumed a full day of team effort. Non-technical stakeholders had no path to self-serve.

The solution

Philip designed and deployed a semantic data layer on top of Databricks — defining metric frameworks, business logic, and dimension/measure relationships so every stakeholder was working from the same source of truth. He then built an AI-powered reporting pipeline: Python extracts Databricks data, an LLM writes the commentary, and the output auto-populates Google Slides and posts directly to Slack.

The outcome

Weekly reporting dropped from a full day to under one hour. Ad hoc analysis turnaround went from hours to under 30 minutes. Non-technical teams could self-serve without analyst involvement — freeing the team to focus on deeper analysis and strategic work.
