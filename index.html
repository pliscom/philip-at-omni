Case Study
Millions in underpayments uncovered - and guiding Woolworths through one of Australia's most complex remediations

PwC · Woolworths Group · Sydney, Australia

Read coverage of the case in the AFR →

The challenge

Woolworths suspected systemic underpayment issues affecting thousands of employees across multiple pay classifications. The root cause was years of legacy systems and software migrations, each one leaving data issues in its wake. The true scale was impossible to see without a dedicated analytical effort, and legal, HR, finance, and data teams all needed to work together to untangle it.

The discovery

Philip led discovery sessions to map Woolworths' data landscape - understanding what systems they had, how teams operated, and where the data lived. Weekly working sessions with middle management, data teams, lawyers, and program leads shaped the analytical approach and built alignment across a complex stakeholder group from day one.

The engagement

What started as weekly findings presentations evolved into daily standups as the work intensified. Philip analysed millions of rows of payroll data using SQL and built the underlying models in SQL Server, while staying on-call throughout - jumping into ad hoc calls to problem-solve in real time and keeping all stakeholders aligned as the complexity grew.

The delivery

When the underpayment results were ready, Philip built a layered dashboard - high-level numbers for senior stakeholders, granular drivers for the data and legal teams. One dashboard, every audience, no confusion. The room understood the scale of the problem immediately.

The outcome

Millions of dollars in underpayments uncovered. Thousands of employees owed remediation. The engagement stood up to legal scrutiny and became the foundation for Woolworths' remediation program.
